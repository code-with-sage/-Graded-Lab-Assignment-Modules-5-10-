The script first checks whether the input file exists to avoid execution errors.
The tr command is used to replace non-alphabetic characters with newlines, converting the text into one word per line and normalizing case.
The longest and shortest words are identified by sorting the words and selecting the appropriate entries.
The average word length is calculated by summing the length of each word and dividing it by the total word count.
Unique words are determined by sorting the list of words, removing duplicates using uniq, and counting them with wc -l.
Pipes are used throughout the script to pass output from one command to the next without creating intermediate files.
